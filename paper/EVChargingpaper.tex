\begin{abstract}
This paper presents a three-layer hierarchical dis-
tributed framework for optimal electric vehicle charging schedul-
ing (EVCS). The proposed hierarchical EVCS structure includes
a distribution system operator (DSO) at the top layer, electric
vehicle aggregators (EVAs) at the middle layer, and electric
vehicles (EVs) charging stations at the bottom layer. A single-
loop iterative algorithm is developed to solve the EVCS problem
by combining the alternating direction method of multipliers
(ADMM) and the distribution line power flow model (DistFlow).
Using the single-loop structure, the primal variables of all
agents are updated simultaneously at every iteration resulting
in a reduced number of iterations and faster convergence. The
developed framework is employed to provide charging cost
minimization at the EV charging stations level, peak load shaving
at the EVAs level, and voltage regulation at the DSO level. In
order to further improve the performance of the optimization
framework, a neural network-based load forecasting model is
implemented to include the uncertainties related to non-EV
residential load demand. The efficiency and the optimality of
the proposed EVCS framework are evaluated through numerical
simulations, conducted for a modified IEEE 13 bus test feeder
with different EV penetration levels.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Charging Scheduling Model Constraints}
\noindent Based on the piecewise linear model, the battery dynamics can be described as follows
\begin{align}\label{0000}
soc_{n,t+1}=soc_{n,t}+\frac{q_n^+p_{n,t}}{C_n}\Delta t-\frac{\omega_{n,t}}{q_n^-C_n}\Delta t
\end{align}
\begin{align}\label{1111}
  soc_n^{min}\leq soc_{n,t}\leq soc_n^{max}
\end{align}
where $q_n^+,q_n^-\in(0,1]$ represent the energy conversion efficiency for charging and discharging of electric vehicles, respectively.To extend battery life,it is recommended to set $soc_n^{min}$ and $soc_n^{max}$ at $15\%$ and $90\%$ respectively,$p_{n,t}$($kW$)represents the charging rate of$EV n$at time slot $t \in \mathcal{T}$,and $C_n$ denotes $EV n$ In each time slot, the charging rate for each electric vehicle remains constant.

%用$\Gamma_n$表示$EV n$充满电所需的总充电功率。由于排队等待时间的限制，$EV n$ 在$\mathcal{I}_n$ 时间段可能无法充满电，因此$EV n$ 的充电策略$p_n\triangleq (p_{n,t};t\in \mathcal{T})$ 满足如下等式:

Let $\bm{p}\triangleq(\bm{p}_n;n\in\mathcal{N})$ represent the charging strategy for the population of $EV$,The set of permissible charging strategies for $EV n$ is denoted as $\mathcal{P}_n$, and the set of permissible charging strategies for all electric vehicles is represented by $\mathcal{P}$.
\begin{align}
  \mathcal{P}\triangleq \mathcal{P}_1\times\cdots\times\mathcal{P}_N
\end{align}
The charging strategy is the most significant factor influencing grid investment. To ensure grid safety, the following inequality holds:
\begin{align}\label{maxP}
p_n^{min}\leq p_{n,t}\leq p_n^{max}
\end{align}
where\,$p_n^{min}$and$p_n^{max}$ represent the minimum and maximum charging power of $EV n$,respectively.By determining whether to charge electric vehicles in each time slot, coordinated charging scheduling of electric vehicles is achieved. The charging status of$EV n$ is as follows
\begin{align}
x_{n,t}=\left\{
\begin{array}{cl}
1 & \text{if the $EV$ is charging}\\
0 & \text{otherwise}.\\
\end{array}\right.
\end{align}
The constraints satisfy the following form:
\begin{align}
(1-x_{n,t})p_{n,t}=0
\end{align}
Electric vehicles with urgent charging needs may not be able to obtain sufficient charge due to their short connection time. Therefore, the state of chargeTherefore, the state of charge (SOC)at the end of their charging period, denoted as$SOC_n^{end}$, may differ from the desired SOC, represented as $SOC_n^d$
\begin{align}\label{SOCcondition}
&SOC_n^{end}=min\{SOC_i^C+\frac{p_{n,t}\cdot\bigtriangleup t\times \eta_n}{C_n},SOC_i^d\}\\
&SOC_n^{min}\leq SOC_{n,t}\leq SOC_N^{max}
\end{align}
where $\eta$ represents the charging energy conversion efficiency of$EV n$.
\subsection{Objective Function Optimization}
%\rd{决策变量是什么？$p_{n,1}$ 的具体意义是什么？}
When electric vehicle users require charging, they tend to prefer efficient charging and aim to complete the charging process as quickly as possible within the designated timeframe. However, such preferences can lead to charging imbalances. Charging imbalances may give rise to new demand peaks, making it necessary to adopt the peak shaving and valley filling method mentioned in the literature to balance the load of electric vehicles.
The problem we are facing is how to centrally coordinate the charging of electric vehicles in order to minimize the total system cost, which serves as the objective function.
\begin{align}\label{OBJ}
\mathcal{J}_0(\bm{p})&\triangleq\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}\left\{\right.f_n(p_{nt})
+g_n(\sum_{t\in\mathcal{N}}p_{nt})\nonumber\\
&+\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}h_n(\sum_{t\in\mathcal{T}}p_{nt})
+\|\bm{p}_n\|_0\left.\right\}\nonumber\\
\end{align}
where each term is specifically explained as below.

1)The first term $f_n(u_{nt})$:it represents the total charging cost for the $EV n$.
This is a price-guided sequential charging control method, which utilizes market-based control approach. The scheduling authority broadcasts price signals to the demand side and updates them periodically. The architecture diagram for this approach is illustrated in Figure 1. The total charging cost can be calculated as follows:
\begin{align}
f_n(p_{n,t})=\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}E_tp_{n,t}\Delta t
\end{align}
where $E_t$ represents the electricity price in time period $t$,$p_{n,t}$denotes the charging power of electric vehicle$n$ during time period $t$, and$\Delta t$refers to the time interval.

2)The second term $g_n(\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}p_{nt})$:This is a satisfaction function for the total energy delivered during the charging period. It represents the satisfaction function for the total energy within the charging cycle. According to equation (\ref{satify}), it can be observed that for electric vehicle $EV\,n\in\mathcal{N}$,the user's desire to increase the amount of electricity provided by the distribution grid as much as possible remains constant across time$t\in\mathcal{T}$until the desired energy is achieved within the expected period.
\begin{align}\label{satify}
g_n(\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}p_{nt})=-(\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}p_{nt}-\Gamma_n)^2.
\end{align}
The weight parameter$\varpi_n\in(0,\varpi_{max}]$ reflects the relative importance of reaching a full charge compared to
$\mathcal{T}_n$.

3)The third term $h_n(\sum_{n\in\mathcal{N}}p_{n,t})$:It represents the ability of peak shaving and valley filling, which aims to maintain the stability of the total power in the grid. Therefore, we use the variance of the total power to reflect its stability. A smaller variance indicates a more stable grid. The total power includes the total power of electric vehicle charging and the total power of residential electricity consumption (denoted as $B_t$), as shown below:
\begin{align}
h(\sum_{n\in\mathcal{N}}p_{nt})=\bl{\frac{1}{T}}\sum_{t\in\mathcal{T}}[(\sum_{n\in\mathcal{N}}p_{nt}+B_{t})-\overline{p+B}]^2
\end{align}
The weight parameter $\omega_n\in(0,\omega^{max})$ and $\overline{p}=(\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}p_{n,t})/T$ 。$\overline{B}=(\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}B_{n,t})/T$

4)The last term $\|\bm{p}\|_0$: The cardinality mentioned here refers to the total number of non-zero elements in the charging strategy $bm{p_n}$.Minimizing the $l_0$ norm represents considering the comfort of EV users to some extent in the overall charging process. On one hand, it aims to minimize the number of charging times and protect the battery's health. On the other hand, EV users prefer to complete their tasks as much as possible because they want to minimize interruptions or restarts of EV charging. Waiting for a longer time to complete tasks is generally unacceptable for most users. Therefore, by introducing a sparse charging plan, the objective of measuring user satisfaction can be unified.

As the process of electrification in the automotive industry progresses, initially, attempts were made to model the charging strategy problem as a minimum $l_2$ norm problem for solving. However, the $l_2$ norm does not capture the sparsity required by the charging strategy of electric vehicles. In contrast, the $l_0$ norm counts the number of non-zero values in a vector. Therefore, the charging strategy is modeled as a minimum $l_0$ norm problem to achieve shorter charging time, improved battery lifespan, and increased user satisfaction. However, the minimum $l_0$ norm problem is NP-hard and cannot be solved using conventional methods. Under certain conditions, the minimum$l_1$ problem and the minimum $l_0$ norm problem are equivalent, meaning that solving the minimum $l_1$ problem can yield the solution to the minimum $l_0$ problem.
Therefore, we approximate the $l_0$ norm with the $l_1$ norm. We rephrase equation(\ref{OBJ}),into the following sparse-promoting charging control model.Formulate the coordination of centralized fast charging for electric vehicles as the following optimization problem:
\begin{align}\label{OBJ_J1}
  \min_{\bm{p}\in\mathcal{P}}\mathcal{J}_1(\bm{p}).
\end{align}
where
\begin{align*}
&\mathcal{J}_1(\bm{p})\triangleq\nonumber\\
&\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}\left\{f_n(p_{nt})
+g_n(\sum_{t\in\mathcal{N}}p_{nt})
+\|\bm{p}_n\|_1\right\}+h(\sum_{n\in\mathcal{N}}p_{nt})
\end{align*}

\subsection{Problem reformulation based on Amproved ADMM}

\noindent$ADMM$方法已成为大规模结构化优化的强大工具.值得注意的是,$ADMM$ 可以解耦空间耦合约束,并将原始问题分解为许多较小的子问题,其中每个子问题都很容易解决.进而达到总体目标的最优.但本文涉及的变量多于两个,因此采用改进的ADMM 算法,并将ADMM扩展为解决具有三个或更多可分离算子的优化问题.具体来说,
 增广拉格朗日公式(\ref{oobj})被拆分为交替和并行,在每次迭代中,该方法会使用新的拆分模式来解决多个较小且较容易求解的变量$x_j$.首先计算出$x_1$, 然后根据计算结果更新$Lagrange$ 乘子,接着可以并行计算其他变量$x_j$. 这种方式可以提高效率,因为它允许并行处理多个变量,加快整个计算过程.

对于具有线性约束的优化问题，目标函数是$M$ 个无交叉变量的独立函数之和，其数学模型可写为:
\begin{align}\label{oobj}
& minmize~~~~\sum_{i=1}^M l_i(x_i)\\\nonumber
& subject~to~~\sum_{i=1}^MA_ix_i=c
\end{align}
其中$l_i$是非严格凸函数,$M\geq3$.$L$为等式约束的个数,$b\in \bm{R}^L$.


\begin{algorithm}
    \caption{Jacobi-Proximal ADMM}
    \label{alg:2}
    \begin{algorithmic}
        \STATE {Initialize: $\bm{p}_i^0(i=1,2,\cdots,M)$and $\lambda^0$}
        \FOR{k=0,1,...}
                \STATE Update~$\mathbf{p}_i$~for~$i=1,...,M$~in parallel by:
                \STATE \qquad $\mathbf{p}_i^{k+1}=
                \textrm{ argmin }
                \big\{ l_i(\mathbf{p}_i)
              +\frac{\rho}{2}\|A_i\mathbf{p}_i+\sum\limits_{j\neq i}A_j\mathbf{p}_j^k -c   -\frac{\Lambda^k}{\rho}\|_2^2+\frac{1}{2}\|\mathbf{p}_i-\mathbf{p}_i^k\|_{\Psi}^2\big\}$
        \STATE Update $\Lambda^{k+1}=\Lambda^k-\gamma\rho(\sum_{i=1}^M A_n\mathbf{p}_n^{k+1}-c)$
        \ENDFOR
    \end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

注意到
\begin{align}
&\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}\left\{f_n(p_{nt})\right\}= \sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}E_tp_{n,t}\Delta t=\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}\Delta t\bm{E}\bm{p}_n\\
&\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}\left\{g_n(\sum_{t\in\mathcal{N}}p_{nt})\right\}= -\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}(\sum_{t\in \mathcal{T}}p_{nt}-\Gamma_n)^2\nonumber\\
&=-\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}(\|\bm{p}_n\|_1-\Gamma_n)^2\\
&\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}\left\{\|\bm{p}_n\|_1\right\}=\sum_{n\in \mathcal{N}}\|\bm{p}_n\|_1
\end{align}
where $\bm{E}=(E_1,E_2, \cdots, E_T)^T$.

此外，通过引入记号
$$p_{N+1,t}=\sum_{n\in\mathcal{N}} p_{nt},
  \bm{p}_{N+1}=(p_{N+1,1},\cdots,p_{N+1,T})^T
$$
我们有：
\begin{align}
  h(\sum_{n\in\mathcal{N}}p_{nt})&=\frac{1}{T}\sum_{t\in\mathcal{T}}[(\sum_{n\in\mathcal{N}}p_{nt}+B_{t})-\overline{p+B}]^2
\nonumber\\

\end{align}
这里$D(\cdot)$表示方差.

因此我们有(15)式的等价形式：
\begin{align*}
  \textrm{minmize}_{\bm{p}\in\mathcal{P}} \quad&\theta_1(\mathbf{p}_1)+\cdots+\theta_N(\mathbf{p}_N)+\theta_{N+1}(\mathbf{p}_{N+1}),\\
  \textrm{subject to } \quad&\mathbf{p}_1+\cdots+\mathbf{p}_N-\mathbf{p}_{N+1}=\mathbf{0},
\end{align*}
where
\begin{align*}
 &\mathcal{J}_1(\bm{p})=\sum\limits_{n=1}^{N+1}\theta_n(\bm{p}_n),\\
 &\theta_n(\bm{p}_n)=\Delta t\bm{E}\bm{p}_n -(\|\bm{p}_n\|_1-\Gamma_n)^2+\|\bm{p}_n\|_1,\nonumber\\
&\theta_{N+1}(\bm{p}_{N+1})=D(\bm{p}_{N+1}+B_{t}).\\
\end{align*}

这里引入指示函数$\mathcal{Z}$
\[
\mathcal{I}_{\mathcal{P}_n}(\bm{p}_n) =
\begin{cases}
0 &\quad \text{if } \bm{p}_n\in\mathcal{P}_n\\
 \infty &\quad\text{otherwise.} \\
\end{cases}
\]
并且令
\begin{align}
\Theta_n(\bm{p}_n):&=\theta_n(\bm{p}_n)+\mathcal{I}_{\mathcal{P}_n}(\bm{p}_n), \forall 1\leq n\leq N,\\
\Theta_{N+1}(\bm{p}_{N+1}):&=\theta_{N+1}(\bm{p}_{N+1})
\end{align}

\begin{align}\label{obj_1}
 \mathop{min} \quad \sum\limits_{n=1}^{N+1}\Theta_n(\bm{p}_n),\\

\end{align}




To utilize\,$Amproved\,\,ADMM$,we define
\begin{align}
\bm{P}=[\bm{p}_1,...,\bm{p}_N,\bm{p}_{N+1}]^{T}\in\mathcal{R}^{(N+1)\times T}
\end{align}



The augmented lagrangian:
\begin{align}
\mathcal{L}_{\rho}(\bm{p},\bm{z},\bm{u})=\sum_{n=1}^{N+1}\Theta_i(\bm{p}_i) +\frac{\rho}{2}\|\sum_{i=1}^Np_i-p_{N+1}+u\|_2^2
\end{align}
where$\rho>0$ is the standard penalty parameter, $u$is referred to as the scaling dual variable or scaling Lagrange multiplier.The variables $p$\, and \,$u$\, are updated simultaneously by adding additional approximation terms. The specific update procedures are as follows:
\begin{align}
&\bm{p}_n^{k+1}:=\mathop{\arg\min}\limits_{\bm{p}\in\mathcal{P}}\Theta_n(\bm{p}_n)\\\nonumber&+\frac{\rho}{2}\|\bm{p}_n^k+\sum_{n\neq j}p_j^k-\bm{u}^k\|_2^2+\frac{1}{2}\|\bm{p}_n-\bm{p}_n^k\|_\Psi^2\\
&  \Lambda^{k+1}=\Lambda^k+\gamma\rho(\sum_{n=1}^{N+1} A_n\mathbf{p}_n^{k+1})
\end{align}
where $\Phi$,$\Psi$,$\gamma>0$satisfy
\begin{align}\label{Con1}
  &\Phi\succ \rho(\frac{1}{\mu_1}-1)I,\Psi\succ \rho(\frac{1}{\mu_2}<2-\gamma)I\nonumber\\
  &\mu_1+\mu_2<2-\gamma
\end{align}
for $\mu_1>0$,$\mu_2>0$.(\ref{Con1})is suffucuent for the convergence of the improved ADMM,

满足(\ref{Con1})的选择$\Phi,\Psi,\gamma$ 有很多种方法,最简单的一种方法是$\Phi=\phi I$,$\Psi=\psi I$ 使得
\begin{align}\label{con1}
  &\phi\succ \rho(\frac{1}{\mu_1}-1),\psi\succ \rho(\frac{1}{\mu_2}<2-\gamma)\nonumber\\
  &\mu_1+\mu_2<2-\gamma
\end{align}

Remark 1：需要注意的是,[yin]中的$Jacobian ADMM$ 是并行的,可以直接应用于求解模型,但所有电动汽车需要共享信息,而我们在这里更倾向于每个智能体仅连接到几个邻居。

Remark2：我们提出$Amproved ADMM$ 相对于[Classical] 中的分布式ADMM 的优点如下.首先,[13] 中的ADMM 变量$(P,X,u)$ 必须按顺序更新,类似于经典的集中式ADMM. 这意味着，在迭代 k +1 时,需要首先更新 P 并用它来更新 X, 然后更新$\bm{u}$ 该更新顺序是不可更改的,因此变量不能同时更新.另一方面,我们提出的$Amproved-ADMM$ 算法通过同时以任意顺序更新 $P、X、u$, 显著改进了[Classical] 中的算法.这对于并行和分布式计算加快计算时间非常重要,特别是当代理数量非常大时.其次,并行更新变量的能力使我们提出的DCJ-ADMM 算法的收敛速度为$o(1/k)$, 这明显快于[Classical] 中算法的$O(1/k)$.
